## Abstract

I have always approached human relationships not as a matter of "personality," but as a matter of **"Interface."**

Closed school environments, anonymous chats, the midnight city, stickers on walls, Zine culture, and live coding—for me, these are all different answers to the same questions:

  * **How can we connect without breaking one another?**
  * **How can we sustain relationships without carrying too much context?**
  * **How can we treat the city and physical space as communication channels?**

Everything I build revolves around these inquiries.

Fueled by the raw self-consciousness of a 17-year-old, I write "embarrassing poems" using the language of Computer Science. The interfaces and protocols I design are my own survival strategies—navigating the fluctuating strength of my own **"A.T. Field,"** struggling to find the right distance from others.

I acknowledge that my work is often naive and overly poetic. Yet, I am committed to converting that poetry into **Structure** and building it into something functional that others can use.

-----

## Core Thesis

For me, Computer Science is more than a means of development; it is a language for re-designing how we interact with the world.

People don't suffer from loneliness because words don't reach them. They break because they are **forced to connect without appropriate interfaces.**

When I design a UI or a protocol, I look at the **"Relational Load"** before the aesthetics. Anonymity, asynchronicity, context decoupling, local storage, and physical-world dependency—these are not just features; they are architectures designed to protect human connections.

-----

## Research Direction

### 1\. Human Relations as Interface

I don't necessarily dislike human contact; rather, I am exhausted by poorly designed interactions. I am deeply interested in anonymity and the "severing of context." This is not an escape, but an experiment to find low-load methods of connection.

### 2\. Treating the City as a Node

I don't see the city as just a location. Street stickers, night parks, and alleyways hold data in a way servers never will. I want to move beyond metaphors and implement systems where data is embedded in, retrieved from, and redistributed through the physical world.

### 3\. Design Beyond Cynicism

I used to use "cynicism" as a defense mechanism to keep the world at a distance. But while cynicism is convenient, it halts connection. I believe we need to build connection methods where **emotions remain intact without being destroyed by the system.** This is why I focus on privacy-preserving, local-first, and asynchronous designs.

## Projects

### 1\. Sticker Bomb App

  * **Concept:** Using physical street stickers as decentralized data carriers.
  * **Function:** Generates QR stickers that store/exchange data locally. It’s a physical-world Nostr client for swapping profiles and art without a central authority.
  * **Vision:** Treating the physical city as a hosting provider for a distributed network.

### 2\. Stargazing Club (The Protocol of Silence)

  * **Concept:** An anonymous school club designed to lower "social pressure."
  * **Logic:** By sharing the non-human scale of the night sky, we reduce the friction of human egos.
  * **Rules:** Telegram-only, no real names, no grades/classes, strictly focusing on the shared view of the sky. A protocol for connecting in real space while remaining anonymous.

### 3\. draw; (Algorithmic Banquet)

  * **Concept:** A research group generating visuals and sound through math.
  * **Function:** Live coding with GLSL and TidalCycles. Viewing visuals not as a "recorded result," but as a "computational process."
